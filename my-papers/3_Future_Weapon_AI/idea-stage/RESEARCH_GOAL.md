# Research Goal: Future Weapon AI — Autonomous Systems, Lethal Autonomy, and the AI Arms Race

## Core Question
How is AI transforming military capabilities, what are the strategic and ethical implications of lethal autonomy, and what governance frameworks could manage the emerging AI arms race?

## Scope
This is a **survey/position paper** (not an empirical study). It synthesizes existing evidence on:
1. The spectrum of military AI (logistics → autonomous platforms → lethal autonomous weapons)
2. Current state of AI in military applications across domains (air, sea, land, space, cyber)
3. The lethal autonomy debate (meaningful human control, accountability, strategic stability)
4. The AI arms race dynamics between major powers (US, China, Russia, Israel, Turkey)
5. Ethical and legal frameworks (IHL, CCW, just war theory)
6. Governance pathways (prohibitions, regulation, confidence-building measures)

## Target Audience
Security studies scholars, policy researchers, defense analysts, ethicists, and international law practitioners.

## Distinguishing Features
- **Distinguish three phenomena:** (a) narrow AI in military support, (b) autonomous platforms with human oversight, (c) fully lethal autonomous weapons
- **Explicit caveats on predictions:** All timelines are "informed speculation"
- **Use defense/military sources:** DoD reports, RAND, IISS, SIPRI
- **Distinguish announced capabilities from verified deployment**
- **Ethical framing:** Just war theory, moral philosophy of autonomous killing, IHL
