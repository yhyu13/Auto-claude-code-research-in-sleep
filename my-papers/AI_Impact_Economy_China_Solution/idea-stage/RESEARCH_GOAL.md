# Research Goal: AI Impact on Economy — China Solution

## Core Question
How is AI reshaping the global economy, and what distinctive policy-institutional pathway is China developing to capture AI-driven growth while managing disruption?

## Scope
This is a **survey/position paper** (not an empirical study). It synthesizes existing evidence on:
1. Global AI economic impact (productivity, labor, GDP, inequality)
2. China's AI ecosystem constraints and advantages
3. China's specific policy instruments and institutional innovations (the "China Solution")
4. Comparative analysis with US and EU models
5. Critical assessment of what works, what doesn't, and what evidence is thin

## Target Audience
Policy researchers, economists, technologists, and China watchers interested in the intersection of AI governance and economic strategy.

## Distinguishing Features (Lessons from Previous Paper)
- **Distinguish three phenomena:** (a) AI as GPT affecting macro-economy, (b) sector-specific disruption, (c) China's geostrategic/industrial policy response
- **Explicit caveats on predictions:** All timelines are "informed speculation"
- **Use Chinese sources:** Include State Council plans, MIIT guidelines, 14th Five-Year Plan sections
- **Distinguish announced policy from verified implementation**
- **Philosophy/institutional framing:** Reference Chinese governance literature, not just Western regulatory frameworks
