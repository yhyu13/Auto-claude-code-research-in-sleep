What This Survey Covers

    • Narrow task automation (tools)

1. The Traditional Research Pipeline

Observation → Background Research → Hypothesis → Experiment
→ Analysis → Publication → Peer Review → Revision

The pipeline is a deliberate engineering abstraction — not an ontological claim.

Real discovery is non-linear, intuitive, and social (Kuhn, 1962; Hanson, 1958).

The Repetition Problem

PhaseTime BurdenRepetitiveness
Literature ReviewWeeks–monthsVery High
ImplementationDays–weeksVery High
Experiment DesignDays–weeksHigh
Writing & ReviewWeeksHigh

A systematic literature review can take months of manual work. These tasks scale linearly with human effort.

2. Three Levels of AI Automation

Tools (Level 1) → Agents (Level 2) → End-to-End (Level 3) → Full Autonomy (Level 4)

Level 1: Narrow Tools

  • LitLLM, Paper Copilot, AutoML
  • 30–80% time reduction per task
  • Require human orchestration

Level 2: Agent Assistants

  • Agent Laboratory, ChemCrow, Coscientist
  • Contiguous sub-workflows
  • Human still in the loop

Where AI Delivers the Highest ROI

  1. Literature synthesis — 5–10× speedup
  2. Hyperparameter / architecture search — 3–10× speedup
  3. Code generation & debugging — 2–5× speedup
  4. Writing & formatting — 2–3× speedup
  5. Internal review — 2–4× speedup

3. State-of-the-Art: End-to-End Systems

The AI Scientist

  • Ideation → Experiments → Writing → Review
  • ~$15 per paper
  • v2: agentic tree search, no templates
  • 42% experiment failure rate
  • Citations often hallucinated

ICLR 2025 Experiment

  • 3 AI-generated papers submitted blind
  • 1 scored above acceptance threshold
  • Authors withdrew for ethical transparency
  • Quality threshold ≠ social acceptance

The Strongest Evidence: AI Already Doing Real Science

Not paper-writing agents — domain-specific scientific AI

AlphaFold

Solved the 50-year protein folding problem. Structure prediction at experimental accuracy. Biologists use it as a standard tool worldwide.

GNoME

2.2M crystal structure predictions. 380,000 novel and stable. Materials were subsequently synthesized in the lab.

AlphaProof / AlphaGeometry

Silver-medal performance at the IMO. Mathematics is where "AI doing science" is most rigorously verifiable — a proof is either correct or not.

Without programmatic verification (CrossRef, PubMed, arXiv API), AI research risks polluting the scientific record with fabricated references.

Critical Gap #2: Plausible Unsupported Success

"The central risk is not only outright failure, but plausible unsupported success — results may be real yet misreported, claims may outrun the evidence that licenses them."
— Lee et al., 2026
  • AI Scientist generated "significant" results via automated p-hacking (no multiple-comparison correction)
  • Wrote convincing but unfounded explanations for why methods worked
  • Attributed success to mechanisms that were never tested

More Critical Gaps

Same-Model Bias

Using GPT-4 to review GPT-4 output creates correlated errors. True independence requires cross-family review (Kimi + MiniMax, Claude + Gemini).

Theoretical Shallow-ness

LLMs are statistical pattern matchers. They interpolate between known solutions but struggle with extrapolation, abstraction, and causal reasoning.

Domain Restriction

End-to-end systems work in ML/data science where experiments are cheap (GPU hours). Wet-lab automation remains distant.

Responsibility Gap

If flawed AI research informs policy or drug design, who is responsible? The machine lacks moral agency.

The Evaluation Problem

Current benchmarks measure task completion (did it run? did it write a paper?).
They do not measure scientific value.

Scientific value requires:

  • Novelty — Is it new?
  • Importance — Does it matter?
  • Reproducibility — Can others verify?
  • Correctness — Are claims supported?
  • Generality — Does it generalize?

No existing benchmark captures all five dimensions.

Trajectory: A Structured Projection

Phase 1 (NOW):       Narrow automation — individual tasks, human orchestrates
Phase 2 (2025-2028): Agentic workflows — sub-pipelines with human checkpoints
Phase 3 (2028-2032): End-to-end automation — full pipeline in comp. domains
                     with verified citations and cross-model review
Phase 4 (2032+):     Full scientific autonomy — theory, robotics, synthesis

These are informed speculations, not forecasts. Phase 4 assumes limitations are engineering problems — this assumption may be wrong.

The Human Role (Even at Full Automation)

  • Goal-setters — defining what problems are worth solving
  • Ethical guardians — ensuring alignment with human values
  • Interpretive partners — making sense of unexpected findings
  • Quality arbiters — judging meaning, not just statistical significance

Key Takeaways

  1. AI dramatically reduces repetition in research (5–10× in literature synthesis)
  2. End-to-end agents exist but have severe quality gaps
  3. The strongest evidence is domain-specific AI (AlphaFold, GNoME, AlphaProof) — not paper-writing pipelines
  4. Critical gaps (hallucination, bias, theory, evaluation) may be fundamental
  5. Evaluation frameworks are as important as the systems themselves

Conclusion

The question is not whether research can be fully done by AI, but under what conditions, in what domains, and with what safeguards.

The answer will emerge gradually, domain by domain,
as verification mechanisms and robotic experimentation mature.
